# Open Platform

PlayOS is an **open platform**: the specification, the reference
implementations, the tools, and the cloud services are open source. No
single entity controls the platform, and no developer is locked in.

## What "open" means

| Aspect | Commitment |
|---|---|
| **Specification** | The PlayOS Book is publicly available under an open license. |
| **Reference implementations** | The Platform API, Runtime, Shell, Cloud, Marketplace, and Tools are open source (MIT or Apache 2.0). |
| **No royalties** | No per-unit fee, no revenue share required to use the Platform API. Stores may charge their own fees. |
| **No lock-in** | Applications use the Platform API, not proprietary SDKs. They can be ported to any platform that implements the API. |
| **Community governance** | The PlayOS Foundation stewards the specification. RFCs and ADRs are public. Contributions are welcome. |
| **Multiple stores** | Anyone can run a PlayOS-compatible store. The official marketplace is one option, not the only option. |

## What "open" does not mean

- **Not a free-for-all** — certification ensures quality and security.
  An open platform can still have standards.
- **Not anti-commercial** — developers can sell games, stores can charge
  fees, OEMs can sell hardware. Open does not mean non-commercial.
- **Not open-core** — there is no "community edition" with missing
  features. The full platform is open.
